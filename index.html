Plainly.

Living with RRMS

I was diagnosed with relapsing-remitting multiple sclerosis, and if you're wondering whether that's something I mind you knowing — I don't. I'd rather say it plainly than have it whispered around.

RRMS is the most common form of MS. The immune system attacks the protective coating around nerves, and it comes in waves: relapses, then stretches of remission. Some days are harder than others. It changes how I pace things. It hasn't changed what I build, or why.

I mention it here because hiding it never made sense to me. If you've just been diagnosed and somehow landed on this page: it isn't the end of anything. Learn about it, find your people, and keep making things.
